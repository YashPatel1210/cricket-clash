# 04 - Weighted Randomness

## Purpose

The draft should not feel completely random.

Instead, randomness should gradually adapt to the current team.

---

# Philosophy

Early draft rounds should be highly random.

The game should not immediately offer the strongest possible players.

This creates excitement and replayability.

---

# Adaptive Draft

After approximately five draft selections,

the system evaluates the current team.

If the team confidence is low,

future drafts receive additional weighting.

---

# Example

Early Draft

Random Playing XI

↓

User creates weak batting lineup

↓

Team confidence becomes low

↓

Generator slightly increases probability of stronger batters appearing

---

# Weighted Factors

Examples:

Current Team Rating

Missing Roles

Recent Draft Picks

Overall Team Confidence

Current Player Form

---

# Goals

The algorithm should:

- Prevent hopeless teams
- Preserve randomness
- Encourage balanced squads
- Never guarantee superstar players

Weighted randomness adjusts probability,

not outcomes.
